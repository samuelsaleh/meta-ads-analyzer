# 1 - Notion Advertising Analysis: France Market

We're starting our analysis of Notion's advertising strategy in the French market during the final quarter of 2025. This competitive intelligence report focuses specifically on their Meta ad activity to understand their B2B acquisition playbook. We want to identify their key messaging, creative formats, and overall testing strategy. This analysis will provide actionable insights into how a major competitor is positioning its enterprise offering. Let's dive into the executive summary to see the scale of this campaign.

# 2 - Executive Summary

The key takeaway here is the sheer volume of testing: Notion deployed 150 ads using 35 unique creative variations in just two months. This campaign was laser-focused on acquiring enterprise users through a strong promotional offer: three months free of Notion Business. They used a systematic approach, testing four core messaging strategies and twelve different headlines to find what resonated best. The campaign ran across both Facebook and Instagram, indicating they view both platforms as critical for B2B reach. This systematic testing approach is further clarified when we look at the campaign metrics.

# 3 - Campaign Overview Metrics

We see a high-volume, rapid-fire testing strategy designed to quickly identify winning combinations before scaling. The 150 ads and 35 unique creatives show a significant investment in A/B testing. The most telling insight is the low impression count on many ads, which confirms they are aggressively testing combinations before committing budget to a few winners. This is a data-driven optimization strategy, not a broad awareness play. Now let's look at how they distributed that testing across different ad formats.

# 4 - Ad Format Distribution

Despite the high volume of testing, Notion heavily favored static images, which accounted for 91% of all creatives. This makes sense because static images are faster and cheaper to produce and iterate for A/B testing. They reserved the more expensive video format for strategic, high-impact content like customer testimonials. This dual strategy allows for efficient testing with images while using video only for proven, high-conversion narratives. The format choice is clearly tied to their testing objective, but what about the actual words they used?

# 5 - Primary Messaging Strategy

Notion tested four distinct value propositions to see which one drove the highest conversion rate for the enterprise offer. The most common message was the Strategic Investment angle, which anchors the value proposition on the free AI suite and zero financial commitment. They also tested urgency-driven messaging and softer brand story narratives, but the focus remained on the financial and feature benefits. This shows a clear effort to optimize the top-of-funnel message before users even click. Next, we'll examine the specific headlines they used to execute these strategies.

# 6 - Headline Creative Variations

We just reviewed the four primary messaging strategies. Now let's look at how those messages translate into the 12 distinct headlines they tested. The key takeaway here is how Notion systematically pairs AI capabilities with tangible business outcomes. They don't just talk about AI; they link it directly to productivity and collaboration benefits. Notice the strong monetary anchoring in headlines like Get up to $12,000 of Free Notion. This creates a high perceived value for the enterprise buyer. This consistent pairing of tech features with clear value shows a highly strategic approach to B2B communication. Now let's see how these messages are visually reinforced.

# 7 - Visual Creative Themes

Following the messaging, we see the visual themes reinforce the same strategic points. Notion uses a consistent visual identity with yellow and teal color schemes to drive brand recognition. The yellow backgrounds are specifically used to highlight value offers like the free trial. They prominently feature the $12,000 of Free Notion value proposition directly on the creative. This visual anchoring ensures the high-value offer is immediately clear, regardless of the headline or primary text. The use of Notion mascots also keeps the brand approachable even when targeting enterprise users. Next, we'll look at when this activity peaked.

# 8 - Campaign Timeline Analysis

We've analyzed the creative elements; now let's look at the deployment schedule. This timeline analysis reveals a significant ramp-up in activity during November. Notion started slowly in October with just two ads, but then launched 30 unique ads in November. This peak activity in November strongly suggests an alignment with Q4 business planning cycles. They were likely pushing hard to capture year-end budget decisions from enterprise clients. The low activity in December suggests they found their winners and began scaling back the testing volume. This deployment pattern confirms a strategic, rather than continuous, testing approach. Now let's examine the final piece of the funnel: the call-to-action strategy.

# 9 - Call-to-Action Strategy

We've seen the campaign ramp up in November. Now let's look at how they drove action. Notion uses a highly simplified CTA strategy, relying almost entirely on "Get offer" to drive conversions. This approach minimizes decision friction for the user. They reserve "Watch Video" only for their video ads, which are typically longer-form testimonials or educational content. Crucially, all ad clicks are routed through the fb.me redirect service. This is a smart move because it allows them to centralize conversion tracking and rapidly A/B test landing page variations without changing the ad creative itself. It shows a focus on optimizing the post-click experience. This disciplined funnel approach is a key part of their systematic testing. Let's summarize what we've learned and look at the strategic implications.

This campaign reveals a systematic, data-driven approach to enterprise acquisition in the French market. The core insight is that Notion prioritizes creative velocity. They use static images for broad testing and reserve video for high-trust social proof. This is efficient.
